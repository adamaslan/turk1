# 10 000 Mots en Francais: A 100-Episode Vocabulary Podcast

## Master French Through Music, Art, Dance, Finance, Nature & Travel

**Method:** Tiered Distribution -- 20 Anchor Words + 80 Satellite Words per episode.
Each episode is a mental folder. Anchor words are high-frequency essentials you will encounter constantly. Satellite words orbit the theme and reinforce the anchors through context, collocations, and usage patterns.

**Why This Works for French:**
- French has ~130,000 active words, but the top 10,000 cover ~95% of all written and spoken text.
- Thematic clustering mirrors how the brain stores vocabulary -- by scene, not by alphabet.
- Each episode includes pronunciation traps (liaisons, silent letters, nasal vowels), gender patterns, and register notes (formal *vous* vs. casual *tu* contexts).
- Cultural notes ground every word in real Francophone life -- Paris, Montreal, Dakar, Brussels, Marseille.

## Appendix A: Episode Structure

Each 30-minute episode follows this format:

1. **L'ouverture** (2 min): A mini-story or scenario using the episode's words in natural context
2. **Les mots cles** (8 min): 20 anchor words -- pronunciation, definition, gender, example sentence, common collocations
3. **L'expansion** (10 min): 80 satellite words in thematic clusters, with quick definitions and usage examples
4. **Le piege** (3 min): Pronunciation traps, gender traps, false friends, and common learner mistakes for this episode's vocabulary
5. **La pratique** (5 min): Fill-in-the-blank, synonym match, scenario response, mini-dialogue
6. **Le mot du jour** (2 min): One beautiful or untranslatable word to carry with you

## Appendix D: Pronunciation Quick Reference

| Challenge | Rule | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Silent final consonant | Most final consonants are silent | *petit* → /puh-TEE/ |
| CaReFuL rule | Final C, R, F, L are usually pronounced | *avec, pour, neuf, hotel* |
| Nasal vowels | Vowel + n/m (not followed by vowel) = nasal | *bon, vin, an, brun* |
| Liaison | Final consonant links to next word's vowel | *les amis* → /lay-za-MEE/ |
| The French R | Guttural, from back of throat | *rue, rouge, rare* |
| U vs. OU | Lips rounded tight (u) vs. relaxed (ou) | *tu* vs. *tout* |
| E caduc | Unstressed *e* often dropped in speech | *je ne sais pas* → *chais pas* |
| H muet vs. H aspire | *H muet* allows liaison; *h aspire* does not | *les hommes* vs. *les heros* |
